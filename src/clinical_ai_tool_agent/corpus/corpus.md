# Educational Clinical Knowledge Snippets

Each line is a single retrievable fact. Edit freely; the FAISS index
will pick them up on next ingest. **All entries are educational
simplifications, not clinical guidance.**

Fever and cough: assess severity. Red flags include hypoxia (SpO2 < 94%), altered mental status, hemodynamic instability.

Mean arterial pressure (MAP) approximates organ perfusion; lower MAP can imply hemodynamic compromise.

Anion gap = Na - (Cl + HCO3). Elevated gap may indicate metabolic acidosis (educational simplification).

When evaluating dyspnea, ask about onset, duration, chest pain, dyspnea at rest, risk factors, comorbidities, and medication use.

When hypoxia is present, consider imaging, ABG, and escalation of monitoring per local protocols (education only).

BMI = weight_kg / (height_m^2). Use carefully and in context, not a diagnostic criterion.

Normal MAP range is roughly 70-100 mmHg in adults; values < 60 mmHg are commonly considered hypotensive in education materials.

A normal anion gap is roughly 8-12 mEq/L (educational reference range; varies by lab).
